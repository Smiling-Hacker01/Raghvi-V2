# Purpose

This document defines who Raghvi is being built for, what problems users face, what jobs they expect Raghvi to perform, and how they will interact with the platform over time.

Every product requirement, architectural decision, and feature should be traceable to one or more user needs described in this document.

---

# Ideal User Profile

The ideal Raghvi user is someone who:

* Relies heavily on digital tools for work or learning.
* Has multiple ongoing responsibilities or projects.
* Frequently switches context throughout the day.
* Wants an assistant that remembers information across conversations.
* Values honest feedback over blind agreement.
* Wants to automate repetitive work while staying in control.
* Is willing to build a long-term relationship with an AI companion.

---

# Primary Persona — The Knowledge Worker

## Overview

Knowledge workers create, organize, analyze, and communicate information as part of their daily work.

This includes:

* Software Engineers
* Students
* Founders
* Researchers
* Product Managers
* Consultants
* Designers
* Technical Writers

---

## Goals

* Stay organized.
* Reduce mental overhead.
* Learn faster.
* Make better decisions.
* Finish meaningful work.
* Manage long-term projects.
* Improve productivity without feeling overwhelmed.

---

## Pain Points

* Information scattered across many tools.
* Too much context switching.
* Forgetting important details.
* Repeating the same information to AI tools.
* Losing track of ideas.
* Difficulty maintaining focus.

---

## Success Criteria

The user feels that:

* Raghvi remembers important context.
* Conversations become more useful over time.
* Less time is spent searching for information.
* Daily work becomes easier and more organized.

# Jobs To Be Done (JTBD)

Users hire Raghvi to perform the following jobs.

---

## Job 1 — Help Me Think

Raghvi helps users reason through problems instead of merely producing answers.

Examples:

* Brainstorm ideas.
* Evaluate trade-offs.
* Explain complex concepts.
* Review plans.
* Challenge assumptions respectfully.

---

## Job 2 — Help Me Remember

Raghvi acts as a trusted external memory.

Examples:

* Remember preferences.
* Track ongoing projects.
* Recall previous conversations.
* Store useful knowledge.
* Connect related ideas over time.

---

## Job 3 — Help Me Act

Raghvi helps users complete work.

Examples:

* Draft messages.
* Open applications.
* Create reminders.
* Manage tasks.
* Execute approved device actions.
* Organize schedules.

All actions require explicit permission where appropriate.

---

## Job 4 — Help Me Grow

Raghvi supports continuous improvement.

Examples:

* Teach new skills.
* Encourage learning.
* Review progress.
* Provide constructive feedback.
* Help users achieve long-term goals.

# User Journey

## Stage 1 — Discovery

The user installs Raghvi because they want more than a chatbot.

Primary goal:

> "I want someone who understands me over time."

---

## Stage 2 — First Conversation

Instead of collecting excessive profile information, Raghvi asks:

> "What would you like me to help you accomplish today?"

The relationship begins by solving a real problem.

---

## Stage 3 — Trust Building

Over multiple conversations, Raghvi gradually learns:

* User preferences.
* Projects.
* Goals.
* Communication style.
* Frequently used tools.

Memory is transparent and user-controlled.

---

## Stage 4 — Daily Companion

Raghvi becomes part of the user's daily workflow.

Examples:

* Morning planning.
* Coding assistance.
* Learning support.
* Task management.
* Evening reflection.

---

## Stage 5 — Long-Term Partner

Months later, conversations become richer because Raghvi understands the user's history, goals, and working style.

The value comes not from remembering everything, but from remembering the *right* things.

# User Pain Points

Users currently experience:

* Repeating information to AI assistants.
* Switching between multiple productivity tools.
* Losing context across projects.
* Difficulty organizing knowledge.
* Generic AI responses.
* Limited automation.
* Lack of personalization.

# User Success Metrics

A successful experience means the user can say:

* "It remembers what matters."
* "It understands how I work."
* "It helps me finish tasks."
* "It gives honest advice."
* "It saves me time every day."
* "I trust it."

# Product Opportunities

Based on this research, the greatest opportunities for Raghvi are:

1. Long-term memory.
2. Honest and balanced reasoning.
3. Android-first task execution.
4. Personalized conversations.
5. Continuous learning support.
6. Cross-project knowledge management.
7. Daily productivity assistance.

---

# Research Summary

Raghvi is not designed to replace search engines or human relationships.

It is designed to become a trusted intelligence platform that helps users think better, remember what matters, and accomplish meaningful work while respecting their privacy, autonomy, and trust.
